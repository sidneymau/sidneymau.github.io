Research

As an undergraduate at UChicago, I have collaborated with scientists from around the world to advance our understanding of dark matter by developing computational tools in high energy particle physics and observational astrophysics.

Survey Science

I have worked with Professor Alex Drlica-Wagner on searching for dwarf galaxies in data from DES, MagLiteS, BLISS and DELVE. I led the discovery and characterization of one new ultra-faint dwarf galaxy, Centaurus I, and two new faint star clusters, BLISS 1 and DELVE 1. ATLAS Experiment

I have worked with Professor Mark Oreglia on searching for invisible decays of the Higgs boson in ATLAS data. I developed a framework for training a neural network on simulated ATLAS data that is now being implemented to increase our sensitivity for detecting exotic decays of the Higgs boson.

TASIS England Physics Research Group

I worked with Dr. Scott C. Dudley on exploring physics using accelerometers and other sensors found in smartphones and similar devices. We also explored using the iOLab system to make novel measurements in the classroom, including mapping the flow of current through a copper sheet.
